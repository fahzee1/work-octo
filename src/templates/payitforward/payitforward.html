Pay it Forward

The Pay It Forward Challenge has been designed to create a learning experience for students while creating a charitable platform for worthwhile local organizations.



about

Since 2011, the Pay It Forward Challenge has tasked marketing and advertising students at major universities with helping local charitable organizations raise funds and awareness.

To jump start the Challenge, students are broken into teams that form mini agencies. Each agency chooses their own local charity and is given the same stipend from Protect America to fund their work.

Once they create their team and have chosen a charity, students start the challenge by drawing up plans, creating awareness and generating funds for their client. Creatively, every team will respond to the challenge differently and create a unique experience. Each team will be able to learn and adapt by facing challenges exclusive to their particular charities.

The Pay It Forward Challenge is a great opportunity for students to put their education into real world practice. Students gain valuable experience in marketing and advertising while helping charities that serve their local community.

Rules

The rules for the challenge are quite simple. The students must gather into teams for the challenge. Each team then creates a marketing and advertising agency that will identify their team. Once they create their team brand and identity, they will pick a charity. Each team will then create marketing campaigns to generate funds and awareness for the charity they partner with.

WHAT ARE YOU GOING TO DO TO CHANGE THE WORLD TODAY?

  • Teams

    Break up into teams, and create your marketing group.

  • Charity

    Pick a charity that you will be helping with your team.

  • Planning

    Make a plan and create ideas to help the charity. You should develop marketing and fundraising strategies with your team.

  • Reporting

    Each team will report their progress in generating awareness and revenue at least once a month. Point Tracking will allow everyone to see where your team stands during the course of the challenge.

  • Presentation

    Turn in your final presentation to your professor displaying your team's accomplishments and creativity during the course of the challenge.
